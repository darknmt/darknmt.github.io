2 Solving $\Delta u = f$ on a Riemannian manifold.

With Kondrachov’s theorem 1, one can uses the familiar "subsequence extracting" technique to find a minimiser of the quadratic functional $\psi \mapsto \frac{1}{2}\int _ M \| \nabla \psi \| ^2 dV$ in a suitable subspace of $W^{1,2}(M)$ (method of Lagrange multiplier), one can prove the following results.

Theorem 2 (Spectrum of $ \Delta $ ). Let $M^ n$ be a compact Riemannian manifold then

  1. The eigenvalues of $\Delta - \nabla ^\nu \nabla _\nu $ are $\geq 0$.

  2. The eigenfunctions of $\delta _0=0$ are constant functions.

  3. The eigenvalue $\lambda _1$ is the minimum value of the functional

    \[ \psi \mapsto \frac{1}{2}\int _ M \| \nabla \psi \| ^2 dV \]

    on the subspace $\left\{ \psi \in W^{1,2}(M): \ \| \psi \| _2 = 1,\int \psi dV=0 \right\} $. Moreover, first eigenfunctions are smooth.

Theorem 3. Given $M^ n$ be a compact Riemannian manifold, consider the Laplace equation on $M$:

1

\begin{equation} \label{eq:lap-M} \Delta u = f \end{equation}

where $f\in L^2(M)$, then:

  1. There exists $u\in W^{1,2}(M)$ satisfying eq:lap-M in the weak sense if and only if $\int _ M f dV = 0$

  2. $u$ is unique up to an additive constant.

  3. If $f\in C^{r,\alpha }$ then $u\in C^{r+2,\alpha }$.
